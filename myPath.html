My Path

From a young age, I’ve always had a natural affinity for science and math. I relish the challenge of problem-solving and the gratification that comes with figuring out how things work. I’ve always known that my true calling lies in a STEM career, and while I initially hesitated to pursue programming, I have since come to realize that it perfectly aligns with my core values. Through experience, maturity, and seizing opportunities, I have developed an unwavering passion for programming, and I am confident that this is the career path that I was meant to pursue. The fundamentals of programming, such as using logic to solve complex problems and completing tasks with precision, give me a sense of purpose and satisfaction that I have never experienced before. Moreover, I thrive in the collaborative environment that programming demands, relishing the opportunity to work alongside like-minded individuals who share my passion for innovation and progress.

Timeline

During my pre-high school years, I explored my interest in STEM by enrolling in applied technology and engineering courses.
In my freshman year of high school, I took electronics with Mr. Kilgore, which helped me explore technology and electrical engineering.
In my sophomore year, I enrolled in my first level of Web Design and I gained an interest in it. I was so fascinated by it that I applied for the Web Design Advisory Board.
The following year, I entered my second level of Web Design where I am currently learning the fundamentals of JavaScript and applying them to website development. I continued to be on the Web Design Advisory Board.
For my senior year, I have decided to take courses such as AP Computer Science Principles, Computer Science A, and the third level of the Web Design program.
At the start of my senior year, I plan to apply to colleges for a software engineering major or other related majors.
